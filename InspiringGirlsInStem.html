Our Speakers

Corrine Welt

Dr. Corrine Welt is a highly accomplished medical professional specializing in the field of endocrinology and reproductive genetics. Dr. Welt completed her medical degree at Cornell University Medical College in New York and went on to complete her internship and residency in Internal Medicine at Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston, MA. She further specialized in Endocrinology and Metabolism through a fellowship at Massachusetts General Hospital, where she also served as faculty until 2014. Dr. Welt joined the University of Utah in 2014 and currently holds the position of Chief of Endocrinology, Metabolism, and Diabetes. Her research interests include the genetics of reproductive disorders with a particular focus on primary ovarian insufficiency and polycystic ovarian syndrome. Her groundbreaking work in this field has contributed to a deeper understanding of the genetic factors underlying these conditions and has paved the way for advancements in diagnosis and treatment.

Pearl Sandick

Dr. Sandick is a distinguished Professor in the Department of Physics and Astronomy and the Associate Dean for Faculty and Research in the College of Science at the University of Utah. She earned her Ph.D. from the University of Minnesota and trained as a postdoctoral fellowship at the University of Texas at Austin. Dr. Sandick is a theoretical particle physicist, studying the fundamental building blocks of matter. Her research focuses on understanding the nature of dark matter in the universe. Outside of research, Dr. Sandick is passionate about teaching, mentoring, and science communication, evident through her engaging TEDx talk and interviews on KCPW's Cool Science Radio and NPR's Science Friday. Throughout her career, she has been a dedicated advocate for women in STEM, both within her local community and on a national scale.

Regan Tracy

Ms. Regan Tracy is a driven and ambitious student at Utah State University, currently pursuing a bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering with an aerospace emphasis and dual minoring in Biomathematics and Electrical Engineering. She is also an undergraduate researcher and manufacturing specialist at the Space Dynamics Laboratory in Logan. She is passionate about creating a more inclusive space for women and minorities in STEM, and is actively working towards this goal as Miss USU, using her platform to advocate for diversity and equal opportunities. Regan's commitment to empowering others extends beyond her title, as she actively participates in her university's Society of Women Engineers (SWE) chapter. Through her various roles and involvements, Regan is making a significant impact in the STEM community and inspiring others to pursue their dreams.

Yekaterina Epshteyn

Dr. Yekaterina Epshteyn is a remarkable individual whose journey from a refugee to a distinguished Professor of Mathematics at the University of Utah is truly inspiring. After completing her undergraduate degree in Applied Mathematics and Physics from the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, she went on to obtain her Ph.D. in Mathematics from the University of Pittsburgh, followed by a postdoctoral fellowship in the Department of Mathematical Science and Center for Nonlinear Analysis at Carnegie Mellon University. With expertise in Computational and Applied Mathematics, her research focuses on solving complex problems in Biology, Materials Science, and Fluid Dynamics. Dr. Epshteyn's exceptional work is supported by grants from the National Science Foundation and Simons Foundation. In addition to her research, she actively participates in various STEM activities and serves as a mentor to high school, undergraduate, and graduate students, helping to nurture the next generation of mathematicians and scientists.

Amanda Hansen

Ms. Amanda Hansen is a Data Analyst at the Space Dynamics Laboratory (SDL), a university affiliated research center associated with Utah State University. With a background in quality assurance, Ms. Hansen's career took an exciting turn as she transitioned into the field of analytics. In her current role, she harnesses the power of data to provide valuable insights, answer critical questions, and drive strategic decision-making processes. Ms. Hansen's educational journey includes a bachelor's degree in Management Information Systems from Utah State University, and a master's degree in Data Analytics from Western Governors University. With her analytical prowess and dedication, Ms. Hansen plays a vital role in shaping the success of SDL and contributing to advancements in the field of data analytics.

Shanti Deemyad

Dr. Deemyad is an experimental condensed matter physicist and Associate Professor of Physics and head of the high-pressure research lab at the University of Utah. With a passion for exploring the quantum effects in lattice and electronic properties of condensed matter systems, she delves into the fascinating realm of exotic states of matter that emerge under extreme conditions of pressure and temperature. Dr. Deemyad's academic journey began at Sharif University of Technology, where she earned her bachelor's degree in physics and conducted research on the effects of oxygen doping on properties of Cuprates. She then pursued her graduate studies at Washington University in St. Louis, earning a PhD under the guidance of Jim Schilling, focusing on the superconducting properties of simple metals under pressure. Following her graduation, Dr. Deemyad completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Harvard University, working with Ike Silvera to study the melting curve of hydrogen under extreme conditions, leading to groundbreaking discoveries. Beyond her research, Dr. Deemyad is a dedicated advocate for the advancement of women in science. She co-founded the "Women of High Pressure" group, which aims to support and empower women within the high-pressure research community. With her expertise, passion for exploration, and commitment to fostering inclusivity, Dr. Deemyad continues to make significant contributions to the field of condensed matter physics and inspire future generations of scientists.

Kendra Schuettpelz

Ms. Kendra Schuettpelz is an exceptional electro-optical engineer known for pushing the boundaries of technology. With expertise in atmospheric and bathymetric lidar, military aircraft, and weapon sights, Kendra's contributions highlight her commitment to advancing cutting-edge technologies. Currently leading the design of a groundbreaking 8kW laser system for testing hypersonic materials, Ms. Kendra Schuettpelz's precision and innovation are reshaping the field. Her dedication extends beyond professional achievements, as seen in her vital role in measuring mirror segments for the James Webb Space Telescope project. With unwavering determination and a track record of success, Ms. Schuettpelz continues to inspire and make a lasting impact in electro-optical engineering.
